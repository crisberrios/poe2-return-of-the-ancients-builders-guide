## Identity

Generic strength/slam powerhouse — "make the small numbers big." Excels at AoE clear via slam aftershocks and big-armour tankiness.

**Signature ascendancy notables** (from [poe2db.tw/us/Titan](https://poe2db.tw/us/Titan)):
- **Hulking Form** — 50% increased effect of Small Passive Skills. Widely regarded as the strongest single ascendancy node in the game.
- **Ancestral Empowerment** — every 2nd Slam Skill is Ancestrally Boosted (deals more damage in a wider area).
- **Mountain Splitter** — every 3rd non-fissure slam creates 3 additional aftershocks.
- **Earthbreaker** — 25% chance for Slam skills to cause an additional Aftershock.
- **Crushing Impacts** — 25% more damage vs Heavy Stunned enemies, hits are Crushing Blows.
- **Stone Skin** — 50% more Armour from equipped body armour. Pairs with The Brass Dome unique.
- **Mysterious Lineage** — 15% more maximum Life.
- **Colossal Capacity** — chest adds 20 inventory slots (utility).

## Patch 0.5 impact on Titan

| # | Patch change | Direction × Magnitude | Why it matters for Titan |
| - | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | **Rolling Slam: first slam 90→75%, second 180→150%; attack time +1.0s (was +1.5)** | **− L** | Hurts leveling and any Titan that uses Rolling Slam for clear. |
| 2 | **Earthquake aftershock: 160→184% to 580→666% at gem 1–20** | **+ M** | Big buff for slam-aftershock Titans (Earthbreaker + Ancestral Empowerment + Mountain Splitter all scale aftershocks). |
| 3 | Boneshatter quality: 0–20% inc. attack speed (was 0–30%) | − S | Small QoL nerf — quality contributes a few %. |
| 4 | Shield Wall / Resonating Shield / Magma Barrier: 5–7 added phys per 15 armour (was 6–8) | − S | ~17% less added damage on shield skills. |
| 5 | Whirling Assault quality: 0–20% **increased** attack speed (was 0–15% **more** attack speed) | − S | "More" → "increased" is a multiplicative-to-additive nerf. Real but small. |
| 6 | Fortifying Cry: limited shockwave per shield-wall segment | − S | Slight nerf to Shield Wall combo. |
| 7 | **Two-hand attack weapon T1 +Level to Melee Skills: +5 (was +7)** | **− L** | Big indirect nerf. Slam Titans relied on these +Level rolls to scale gem damage. ~2 effective levels lost on best-case weapons. |
| 8 | One-hand mace base range 1.0m | + S | Marginal QoL clear buff for 1H+shield Titans. |
| 9 | Stone Runes (Martial Weapons): 20/30/40% stun buildup (was 20/25/30%) | + S | Stun-focused Titans gain noticeably more buildup at T2/T3. |
| 10 | Bonded gloves modifier: 25% warcry CDR (was 15%) | + S | Helps Infernal Cry uptime. |
| 11 | Voracious Notable: no longer 20% instant leech; now 15% attack speed while leeching | − M | Loss of instant leech is a survivability hit; AS-while-leeching is a partial offset for clear builds. |
| 12 | Goring Notable: no longer 40% phys; now 3% max life + 20% leech | + S | Net slightly tankier; lost a damage node. |
| 13 | Ancestral Bond Keystone rework (75 spirit per totem; doubles limit; no cost/charge) | ~ | Largely irrelevant for Titan (totem playstyle is Smith/Warbringer territory). |
| 14 | Player change: bleed-on-self no longer amplified while moving | + S | Pure QoL defensive change. |

### Aggregate (rubric: ±L = ±1.0, ±M = ±0.3, ±S = ±0.1)

**Buffs:** +0.0L · +0.3M · +0.5S = **+0.80**
**Nerfs:** −2.0L · −0.3M · −0.4S = **−2.43**
**Net:** **−1.63** (mild aggregate nerf, driven by Rolling Slam + Two-Hand +Level weapon nerfs)

> Caveat: this rubric weights raw skill/weapon nerfs heavily, but Titan's core ascendancy nodes are untouched. The Earthquake buff specifically helps Titan more than other warriors because of its slam-aftershock-stacking notables.

---

## Net verdict — Titan

- **0.4 tier (Mobalytics):** A.
- **0.5 verdict (this analysis):** A. Probably still in the top 6 ascendancies.
- **Direction:** **flat to slightly down** in absolute power, but **up relatively** because Warrior's nerfs are smaller than what 0.4's S-tier classes (Pathfinder, Blood Mage) got hit with.
- **What got buffed:** Earthquake (#2). Warcry CDR gloves (#10). One-hand mace range (#8). Stun runes (#9).
- **What got nerfed:** Rolling Slam (#1). Two-Hand +Level item rolls (#7). Voracious instant leech (#11). Whirling Assault quality (#5).
- **League-start viability:** **Strong**. Titan retains its "make small numbers big" identity intact (Hulking Form untouched). Slam-aftershock builds get a direct buff. Expect Earthquake Titan and Shield Wall Titan to remain top picks while Rolling Slam variants lose some shine. Headhunter remains the chase item.

**Recommended starter path:** Earthquake or Shield Wall Titan. Skip Rolling Slam-centric variants until the dust settles. Aim for The Brass Dome + Headhunter as your two chase uniques.
